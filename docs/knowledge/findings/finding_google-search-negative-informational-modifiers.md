What was found: `طريقة` ("method/how-to") and `متطلبات` ("requirements") were proposed as phrase negatives in ZATCA/e-invoice campaigns. Both are wrong. `متطلبات` = businesses being legally forced to comply = exact ICP. `طريقة` + product keywords = learning-intent with solution need. `كيفية` ("how to") = same logic. What IS worth negating: `fatoora`, `fatoora platform`, `fatoora portal`, `ZATCA portal` — routes to the government's free compliance portal.

Source: memory/08_pitfalls.md "Google Search negative keyword policy — informational modifiers".

Impact: Negating these would block compliance-mandate audience — our primary ICP.

Fix / How to handle: Only negate informational modifiers that lead to content consumption (دورة/كورس/تحميل/نموذج مجاني) or to a specific competing free product (fatoora/ZATCA portal). Modifiers describing the buyer's problem (requirements, method, how-to) stay open. See `keyword_policy.ALWAYS_NEGATIVE` for the definitive negation list.
