# Ethical Marketing Guidelines

Source: Psychographic and Behavioral Audience Analysis (Notion, Nov 2025)

## The Ethical Spectrum

| Ethical (DO) | Gray Area (CAUTION) | Manipulative (DON'T) |
|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Transparent, helpful | Personalized without disclosure | Hidden, exploitative |
| Netflix recommendations | Filter bubbles | Dark patterns |
| Fitness app nudges | Engagement algorithms | Addiction mechanisms |

## Green Flags (What We Should Do)
- Clear disclosure of personalization
- User control and opt-out options
- Genuine user benefit in content
- Transparent nudging
- Respect for user autonomy
- Privacy by design

## Red Flags (What to Avoid)
- No transparency about targeting
- Exploiting psychological vulnerabilities
- No informed consent for profiling
- Distorting reality or limiting choice
- Addictive design patterns
- Discriminatory practices

## Practical Rules for Qoyod Content

1. **Always provide genuine value** — Every article should teach something useful, not just sell
2. **Be transparent** — If content is promotional, make it clear
3. **Don't exploit fear** — Even for compliance content, inform rather than scare
4. **Respect autonomy** — Present options, don't pressure decisions
5. **Avoid dark patterns** — No fake urgency, fake scarcity, or misleading CTAs
6. **Privacy first** — Don't track or profile users beyond what's necessary

## Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Content provides genuine educational value
- [ ] No misleading claims or fake urgency
- [ ] CTAs are honest about what happens next
- [ ] Personalization is transparent
- [ ] No exploitation of fear or anxiety
- [ ] Compliant with Saudi data protection regulations
